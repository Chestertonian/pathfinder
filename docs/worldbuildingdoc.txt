## Worldbuilding Document (Current State Summary)

This document summarizes the established game world geography, major travel networks, and location ID ranges as developed so far.

---

# 1. Core Urban Hub

### Veranis

* Primary starting city and central population hub.
* Structured urban environment with gates and controlled exit routes.
* Main connection point to all major road systems.

### Key known locations (Veranis region)

* Eastern Gate complex (entry funnel into exterior road network)
* Interior gate courtyard area (transition zone between city and road systems)

---

# 2. Eastern Road System

### Eastern Road

**ID Range: ~4–25**

* Primary imperial artery extending east from Veranis.
* Linear road with occasional minor branching and environmental variation.
* Designed for long-distance travel with sparse but stable infrastructure.

### Structure

* Early segment: immediate outskirts of Veranis (IDs ~4–14)
* Mid segment: extended rural stretch (IDs ~15–25)
* Features:

  * gradual reduction in population density
  * introduction of minor junctions
  * at least one inn located along the route
  * one northern branching connection into secondary road system

---

# 3. Northern Road System

### Northern Road

**ID Range: ~33–41**

* Branches north from the Eastern Road at a mid-point junction.
* More rural, elevated terrain with hills and open sky exposure.
* Lower traffic density than Eastern Road.

### Key features

* Linear progression northward
* One major eastern branch leading into forested terrain
* Terrain shifts from open hills → transitional forest edge

---

# 4. Forest System (Eastern Branch of Northern Road)

### Forest Grid

**ID Range: 55–84 (30 rooms total)**

* Dense forest biome structured as a **5×6 grid system**.
* Entry point: from Northern Road eastern branch (connection at room ~41 → forest entrance at ~57).
* Fully grid-connected via cardinal directions only.

### Structure

* Row A (Edge zone): lighter forest, transitional canopy
* Row B–C (Interior): standard forest density, navigable paths
* Row D (Deep forest): low light, ancient terrain
* Row E (Forest depths): dense, near-isolated environment

### Characteristics

* Fully navigable grid with N/S/E/W connections
* Increasing density and reduced visibility toward deeper rows
* Includes subtle clearings and “natural crosspaths” but no settlements

---

# 5. Southern Road System

### Southern Road from Eastern Crossroad

**ID Range: ~42–49 main spine**

* Branches south from Eastern Road (junction at ~21 in original map context).
* Transition from imperial road network into rural agricultural region.
* More organic settlement pattern, less structured infrastructure.

### Structure

* Linear southern spine with two major divergence points:

  * Southeast agricultural branch
  * Southwest major rural expansion route

### Characteristics

* Increasing farmland density moving south
* Roads become less formally maintained
* Emergent rural economy (cottages, fields, livestock)

---

# 6. Southeast Agricultural Branch

**ID Range: ~50–51 + settlement extension**

* Narrow farmland-focused extension of southern system.
* Light traffic, mostly local agricultural movement.
* Leads to a small farming hamlet.

---

## Harthaven Hamlet (Southeast endpoint)

**ID Range: 85–89**

* Small rural settlement consisting of:

  * clustered cottages
  * barns and livestock areas
  * communal well
  * surrounding farmland

### Characteristics

* Non-urban, low-governance settlement
* Economically tied to surrounding fields and regional roads
* Acts as the endpoint of the southeast rural branch

---

# 7. Southwest Branch

**ID Range: ~52–54**

* Larger and more structurally significant rural route than southeast branch.
* Moves toward more organized farmland and denser rural habitation.
* Acts as the primary expansion direction of southern settlement growth.

---

# 8. World Structure Summary

## High-level layout

* **Veranis**: centralized urban hub
* **Eastern Road**: main linear trade artery
* **Northern Road**: rural elevation corridor with forest access
* **Forest Grid**: dense exploration biome (30-node grid system)
* **Southern System**: agricultural expansion network with branching settlement logic
* **Harthaven Hamlet**: first true rural settlement outside Veranis region

---

# 9. Design Principles Established

* Roads define geography (not the other way around)
* Branching systems represent economic/social divergence:

  * East = imperial structure
  * North = sparse terrain transition
  * South = rural expansion
  * Forest = ecological density zone
* No circular routing loops; world is a directed graph with branching expansion
* Regions are organized by **ID ranges + region tags**, not strict uniqueness constraints on names

---

If you want next evolution steps, the system is now ready for:

* inter-region travel constraints (blocked roads, seasonal access)
* settlement hierarchy (hamlet → village → town progression)
* or dynamic travel costs across biome types
