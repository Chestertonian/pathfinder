## Worldbuilding Document (Current State Summary)

This document summarizes the current geography, travel networks, and major inhabited regions in the game world. It focuses on structure, connectivity, and functional roles rather than detailed internal layouts.

---

# 1. Core Urban Hub

### Veranis

* Primary starting city and central population hub of the region.
* Heavily structured urban environment serving as the main administrative and economic center.
* Acts as the origin point for the major outward road systems.

---

# 2. Eastern Road System

### Eastern Road

**ID Range: ~4–27 (current known extent)**

* Primary imperial trade route extending east from Veranis into rural territory.
* Broad, stable roadway designed for consistent travel and trade movement.
* Gradually shifts from urban outskirts into sparsely populated countryside.

### Key characteristics

* High structural consistency and navigability
* Low settlement density along most of its length
* Occasional travel infrastructure (e.g., inns, rest points, junctions)
* Transition zone from city influence into provincial land

---

# 3. Northern Road System

### Northern Road

**ID Range: ~33–41**

* Branches northward from the Eastern Road.
* Less trafficked and more rural than the main eastern artery.
* Leads into elevated terrain and transitional wilderness zones.

### Key characteristics

* Sparse traffic compared to Eastern Road
* Increasingly natural and less maintained environment
* Connects to forested regions through an eastern deviation

---

# 4. Forest Region

### Forest Grid

**ID Range: 55–84 (30 connected locations)**

* Dense forest region structured as a fully connected grid system.
* Entered via a branch from the Northern Road.
* Represents a self-contained ecological zone with layered density.

### Key characteristics

* High-density natural environment
* Increasing isolation toward deeper interior sections
* Limited traversal visibility and slower movement feel
* Contains no permanent settlements

---

# 5. Southern Road System

### Southern Road system

**ID Range: ~42–54**

* Southern extension branching from the main Eastern Road system.
* Transitions from structured imperial infrastructure into rural and agricultural territory.
* Functions as a major expansion route into less centralized regions.
* Now also serves as the primary access spine leading toward the Grasslands Region via southern offshoots near the mid-southern grid (around Location 13 boundary connections).

### Key characteristics

* Increasingly agricultural and less formally maintained
* Multiple branching routes into rural subregions
* Acts as a gateway into the broader Southern Lands

---

# 6. Southwestern Village

### Brackenridge Village

* Small rural settlement located west of the southwestern road branch.
* Serves as a local agricultural and trade node for surrounding farmland.
* Acts as a minor population center within the southern expansion region.

### Functional role

* Provides goods and services to surrounding rural areas
* Serves as a waypoint between farmland and the broader road network
* Represents the first structured settlement beyond the primary road corridors in this direction

---

# 7. Southeast Hamlet

### Harthaven Hamlet

**ID Range: 85–89**

* Small farming settlement located at the terminus of a southeastern branch from the southern system.
* Functions as a localized agricultural endpoint rather than a transit hub.
* Closely tied to surrounding farmland and seasonal production cycles.

### Functional role

* Local subsistence and agricultural support
* Minor trade and resource exchange with nearby routes
* Acts as a geographical endpoint of its branch system

# 8. Grasslands

### Plains

**ID range: 102-141**

* Comprised of a fully connected grid of open terrain with minimal structural landmarks and no settlements.
* No permanent settlements or constructed infrastructure
* Terrain is uniform grid
* South of eastern road

### Functional role

* Acts as the first major wilderness biome SE of the city.


---

# 8. World Structure Overview

## Macro layout

* **Veranis**: central urban origin point
* **Eastern Road**: primary long-distance trade artery
* **Northern Road**: rural and transitional route into wilderness
* **Forest Region**: dense ecological system accessed via northern branching
* **Grasslands Region**: open wilderness plains south of eastern road systems, accessed via eastern road.
* **Southern Road System**: agricultural expansion network with multiple sub-branches
* **Southwestern Village**: rural settlement node off the southern corridor
* **Southeastern Hamlet**: endpoint farming settlement on a minor branch

---

# 9. Design Principles (Current State)

* Geography is defined through connected travel networks rather than fixed zones
* Settlement density decreases with distance from Veranis
* Roads act as structural anchors for world organization
* Wilderness regions are spatially dense and internally connected
* Branching systems represent economic and environmental divergence rather than administrative borders

---

If you want the next evolution step, the world is now at the point where it can support:

* regional trade logic between settlements
* terrain-based movement modifiers (river, hills, forest density)
* or introduction of a true “Southern Lands” macro-region beyond the current road endpoints
